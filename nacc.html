Nigerian Civil Aviation Authority Reopening Animation

The objective was to create a vibrant, informative animation announcing the reopening of Nigerian skies while promoting COVID-19 safety protocols. The piece combines 2D character animation, kinetic typography, and motion graphics to deliver a reassuring message to travelers.

Problem

Reopening airspace required communicating complex safety protocols quickly without overwhelming travelers or dampening the excitement of returning to flight.

Solution

A fast-paced, 60-second spot using stylized characters and kinetic typography to break down safety measures into digestible, visual "cuts" while maintaining an optimistic tone.

Value Delivered

Established national trust through brand-consistent visual identity and effectively reinforced "personal responsibility" through memorable, customer-focused animation.

Process

Motion Design & Animation

We designed stylized, relatable characters to represent passengers and airline staff, fostering a sense of community. By using smooth, fluid limb movements, we conveyed a sense of joy as air travel resumes.

Environment and prop animations—including planes taking off and terminals—created a sense of movement, while kinetic typography ensured the "stay safe" message was visually reinforced throughout the piece.

Strategic Insights

Production Narrative

The animation begins with a high-energy "hooray" to celebrate that Nigerian skies have reopened. To prevent this excitement from overshadowing safety, the pacing shifts to a more deliberate tempo when introducing protocols.

Managing rapid-fire safety protocols was resolved by using distinct visual cuts for each measure—mask-wearing, distancing, and hygiene—ensuring the information remained customer-focused and easy to follow.
